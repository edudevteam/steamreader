## 🚀 Tutorial

### What is Story Boarding

Think of a **storyboard** like a comic strip for a movie or a book. It's a way to plan out a story using pictures before you start filming or writing the final version.

#### Here is how it works:

- **Drawing the Action:** You draw a series of boxes. Inside each box, you sketch what is happening in that part of the story.

- **Adding Details:** Under each drawing, you write a quick note about what the characters are saying or what sounds are playing.

- **Checking the Flow:** By looking at all the boxes in a row, you can see if your story makes sense from beginning to end.

### Why do people use Storyboards ?

1. **To Save Time:** It's easier to fix a drawing than to re-film a whole movie scene!

2. **To Share Ideas:** It helps show other people exactly what you are thinking.

3. **To Be Organized:** It acts like a map so you don't get lost while telling your story.

## 🧩 Challenge

### Overview

This is a a two part challenge.

- **Part 1**: decide on a topic for your story and storyboard it out. This can be on a piece of paper, whiteboard, or online tool.

- **Part 2**: convert your story into a Sprite Lab App. You will need to apply all the skills you learned in lessons 1 - 4.

### Requirements

1. Come up with an idea for a story
2. Storyboard the idea out. You need a start screen, end screen, and three interactive screens in between.
3. This story can be as simple or as complex as you like.
4. Your completed Sprite Lab project must include the following blocks. You are not limited to only these blocks but these blocks must be used somewhere in your project.
   - ☑️ Sprites
   - ☑️ Variables
   - ☑️ If Blocks
   - ☑️ Events
   - ☑️ Functions
   - ☑️ Behaviors
   - ☑️ Math Blocks
   - ☑️ Location Blocks
   - ☑️ Audio Blocks
