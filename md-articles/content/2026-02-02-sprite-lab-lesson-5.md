### What is Story Boarding

If you are following a tutorial or building something like a two-page/scene story then you probably don't need to story board. But... if you plan to make an interactive story like game often called a `Visual Novel` it is smart to plan ahead.

We start this off by asking: What is our story about? How many main characters are there? Where is the story set? How will the user / player interact with my story? Where am I getting my assets from? Do you I need to create custom assets? etc.

### Tools for Story Boarding

- A sheet of paper
- A white board
- An online tool like:
  - Excalidraw.com (Free)
  - Milanote (Free)
  - Canva.com (Free & Paid)
  - StoryTribeApp.com (Free & Paid)

> Personally, I am fan of either using paper/whiteboard or Excalidraw.com

### Custom Assets

An asset is a term used often in the game development world to mean sprites or backgrounds. Before we talk about how to import custom assets we need some samples.

> **Important** -- If are working from a classroom environment on Code.org Sprite Lab you might not be able to import custom assets. If you can't ask your teacher to provide access.

You have a couple of ways of getting free assets. You can find them online from Google Images to searching free assets or searching exactly the picture you want. Remember there's are two assets. Another solution is using the AI tools to generate the images like Google Gemini, ChatGPT, etc.

Even though you can create custom AI generated images now it is actually better to use existing assets (sprites and backgrounds) that already exist in the Sprite Lab. It makes the development easier.
